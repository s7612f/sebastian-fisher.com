ABOUT

Sebastian Fisher. Started lifting at 15 after finding Arnold's Encyclopedia of Modern Bodybuilding and realising that what I'd been told about fitness — that it wasn't for me — was wrong. Six years under the bar since. Never missed more than a week.

I'm Type 1 diabetic. That forced me to understand how food, training, and the body actually interact in more depth than most people bother with — specifically what protein does to blood sugar, how energy availability affects performance, and why nutrition decisions have consequences that show up in your training whether you track them or not.

Currently training on an Arnold-inspired 3-day split, hypertrophy focus, mid-cut. Working toward a Level 3 PT Diploma through Educate Fitness. Based at JustGym, Saffron Walden.

I coach people who are new to the gym and overwhelmed by conflicting advice, people who've been training for a while but aren't progressing, and anyone who wants someone to look at their programme and diet without the bullshit.

MY APPROACH

No trends. No shortcuts. No protocols named after bodybuilders who used pharmaceutical support that's beyond reach for 99% of people.

TRAINING

Compound movements first. Progressive overload as the primary driver. Volume built up gradually. No exercise rotation every week.

NUTRITION

Hit your protein. Control your calories. Eat real food. Standard, vegetarian, or keto — whichever you'll actually stick to.

CONSISTENCY

The one variable that actually matters more than anything. A mediocre programme followed consistently beats the perfect programme you abandon after 3 weeks.

HONESTY

I'll tell you when something isn't working, when your diet is the problem, and when you're not training hard enough. That's the job.

WHY OLD SCHOOL?

The bodybuilders of the 1960s–80s built the greatest physiques we've ever seen with basic equipment, no internet, no tracking apps, and no pre-workout stacks. What they had was discipline, knowledge of fundamentals, and the willingness to work hard consistently.

Modern fitness is drowning in complexity. New methods, new splits, new research that overturns last year's research. Most of it is noise. The fundamentals haven't changed.

"The basic exercises work. They worked then, they work now. Do them with proper form, progressively heavier over time, and eat enough protein. Everything else is commentary."
