TRAINING METHODS & RESEARCH

Techniques that inform how I programme. Some are staples of every coaching programme; others are research interests I continue to study. Where the evidence backs it, it’s linked.

Progressive Overload

Add one rep or kg at a time. Slow consistent progress beats aggressive jumps that end in injury. Track every session — numbers don’t lie.

► Research: Long-Term Adaptation
Rest-Pause / Myo-Reps

Take 3–5 breaths mid-set, then continue. Extends the set without full recovery. More total volume in less time, keeping the muscle under tension.

► Research: Volume & Hypertrophy
Occlusion Training (BFR) [RESEARCH]

Blood flow restriction — bands above the muscle to trap blood in the working tissue. Research suggests it activates growth factors at lighter loads. Not a standard part of coaching programmes — included here as an active research interest.

► Research: BFR / Occlusion Training
Strip Sets

Reduce resistance mid-set when form breaks down. Never stop the set. Doubles volume within a single working set. Used on compound and isolation work.

► Research: Volume & Hypertrophy
Strength Phase / Mass Phase Periodisation

Alternating blocks: heavy strength (low reps, progressive overload) then hypertrophy (volume, pump, mind-muscle). Prevents plateaus over multi-month programmes.

► Research: Progressive Overload & Adaptation
High Frequency / Low Intensity

Can’t feel a muscle working? Train it more often at light weight. Build the neural pathway before adding load. Specificity before intensity.

► Research: Training Frequency
Cardio at High Intensity

Hard cardio — not casual walking. Reduces myostatin, increases follistatin. Used during cut phases and metabolic conditioning blocks. Not decorative.

► Research: Exercise & Body Composition
Fascia Stretching While Pumped [RESEARCH]

Stretching while the muscle is pumped. The hypothesis is that it expands the fascial sheath. Evidence is mostly anecdotal — included here as a research interest, not a coaching claim.

► Research: Fascia Stretching
Sleep Protocol

Sleep is the most underrated recovery variable. No supplement compensates for chronic sleep deprivation. Protocol includes sleep hygiene, timing, and environment.

► Research: Sleep & Recovery
Stress Management

Chronic psychological stress elevates cortisol, promotes fat gain, suppresses anabolic hormones. Addressed in the 6-week overhaul. Not optional in physique work.

► Research: Stress, Cortisol & Body Composition
