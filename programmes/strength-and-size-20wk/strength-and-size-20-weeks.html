20-Week Powerbuilding Programme
Compound-First · Pyramid Loading · Periodised Intensity

Programme Overview

A 20-week powerbuilding programme that develops maximum strength in the big three lifts (squat, bench, deadlift) whilst building muscle through structured accessory volume. Four progressive blocks, each building on the last, with strategic deload weeks built in at weeks 5, 10, and 15. Week 20 is a test week: attempt 1RM on squat, bench, and deadlift.

Training split: 5 days/week — Upper Strength / Lower Strength / Rest / Push Volume / Pull Volume / Legs Volume / Rest

Session length: 60–80 minutes

Equipment needed: Barbell, squat rack, dumbbells, cables, leg press, pull-up bar

Rep scheme: Pyramid — 5 sets of 15, 12, 8, 8, 6 on main lifts (ascending load)

Deload weeks: 5, 10, 15 — heavy compounds only, 3–5 reps, 50% volume

How to Use This Programme

  • Track every session: sets, reps, weight, RPE. Without a log, progressive overload is guesswork.
  • Pyramid loading: increase weight each set as reps decrease. Set of 15 = feeder set. Sets of 6–8 = working weight.
  • RPE targets given per block. RPE 8 = 2 reps left. RPE 9 = 1 rep left. RPE 10 = nothing left.
  • Deload weeks (5, 10, 15): big three compounds only. Heavy (3–5 reps), 3 sets, no accessories.
  • Week 20 test week: rest Monday and Tuesday, then test 1RM Wednesday (squat), Friday (bench), Saturday (deadlift).
  • Nutrition: caloric surplus 200–400 kcal. Minimum 1.8g protein per kg bodyweight.
  • Sleep: minimum 7 hours. Non-negotiable for strength adaptation.

Signs You Need to Pull Back

  • Compound lifts declining week-on-week (not just an off day).
  • Joint pain that worsens during or after sessions.
  • Persistent fatigue — poor sleep, low motivation, irritability.
  • Resting heart rate elevated 8–10 bpm above your baseline.
  • Technique breaking down on sub-maximal weights.

Block 1: Weeks 1–4 — Foundation

Establish movement patterns, baseline strength, and training habits. Moderate volume, controlled effort. Every session should feel hard but recoverable. Pyramid every main lift: warm up, build, finish heavy. Accessories at fixed rep targets.

Target RPE: 7–8 on working sets (sets of 8 and 6).
Nutrition: 200–300 kcal surplus. Prioritise protein.
Frequency: 5 days on, 2 days off (see split above).

Deload Week 5

One session only this week. Big three compounds + barbell row and OHP. Heavy and low rep — this is not a light week. Cut total volume in half. No accessories. Eat at maintenance. Sleep extra.

When: Any day mid-week. Wednesday or Thursday preferred.
Goal: Nervous system recovery whilst maintaining neural drive on the main lifts.

Barbell Bench Press3 × 3–53 minHeavy press; no accessories
Barbell Row3 × 52 minStrict row; heavy
Overhead Press3 × 52 minStanding; heavy

Deload Rules

  • One session this week — not three. The point is recovery, not maintenance volume.
  • Weights should be heavy relative to feel: 88–92% of your recent top set.
  • No accessories. No cardio beyond easy walking.
  • If still feeling flat after deload, add one more rest day before Block 2.
Block 2: Weeks 6–9 — Volume Ramp

Add a sixth set to main compound lifts. Rep targets tighten on upper/lower strength days. Accessories stay similar but load increases. This is where most of the hypertrophy work happens. RPE climbs week by week.

Target RPE: 8–9 on compound working sets.
Nutrition: Hold 200–400 kcal surplus. Bodyweight should be trending up 0.3–0.5 kg/week.
Auto-regulation: If a weight feels heavier than expected, keep it — don’t drop the load. If a set goes past RPE 9, end the set.

Block 3: Weeks 11–14 — Intensity

Drop volume slightly; increase intensity. Main compound lifts shift to lower rep ranges to build peak strength. Accessories maintain muscle. Each week you should be hitting new rep PRs on the main lifts. RPE 9 on compound working sets is the target.

Target RPE: 9 on main compound working sets.
Technique note: At RPE 9, technique will be tested. Stop the set if form breaks down — a grinder rep with bad form does more harm than good at this stage.

Block 4: Weeks 16–19 — Peak

Maximum intensity. Low volume, high load. Every compound session is an opportunity to set a rep PR. By week 19, your top working sets should be at or near your current 1RM. Week 20 is the test. Accessories are maintenance only — keep them but don’t add load.

Target RPE: 9–10 on final working sets.
Rest periods: Take full rest. 4–5 minutes between heavy sets is not excessive at this point.
Nutrition: Bump to 400 kcal surplus if bodyweight has stalled. Final 2 weeks: eat at maintenance to sharpen up.

Week 20 — Test Week

Attempt 1RM on squat, bench press, and deadlift. Three sessions across the week with full rest between each. No accessories. No other training.

Schedule: Wednesday — Squat  ·  Friday — Bench Press  ·  Saturday — Deadlift
Monday and Tuesday: Full rest. Eat at maintenance. Sleep 8+ hours.
Warm-up protocol: Work up gradually — 50% × 5, 70% × 3, 80% × 2, 90% × 1, then attempt.

AttemptLoadNotes
Warm-Up 150% of estimated 1RM × 5Movement rehearsal
Warm-Up 270% × 3Bar speed check
Warm-Up 380% × 2Feeling out the weight
Opener90% × 1Should feel comfortable — build confidence
Second attempt95–97% × 1Near-max; conservative PR attempt
Third attempt (optional)100%+ × 1Only if second attempt felt solid

1RM Test Protocol

  • Take 5+ minutes rest between every attempt.
  • Have a spotter for squat and bench. No exceptions.
  • If a lift feels off on your opener, stop — don’t push to third attempt.
  • Record your result immediately. This is your new baseline for the next cycle.
  • After test week: deload for 7–10 days, then decide whether to repeat or adjust.

Big Three Technique Notes

Back Squat

CueDetail
Bar positionHigh bar: traps. Low bar: rear delts. Low bar allows more forward lean and heavier loads.
StanceShoulder-width to slightly wider. Toes pointed out 15–30°.
DescentSit back and down. Knees track toes. Depth: hip crease below knee minimum.
BracingBig breath into belly before descent. Hold it through the hole. Exhale at top.
DrivePush the floor away. Chest up. Hips and chest rise at the same rate.

Bench Press

CueDetail
SetupEyes under the bar. Arch in lower back. Retract and depress scapulae. Feet flat.
Grip81cm ring markings as a guide. Wrists straight, not cocked back.
DescentBar to lower chest. Elbows at 45–75° to torso — not flared, not tucked.
DrivePush ceiling away. Drive feet into floor. Bar travels slightly back toward rack on ascent.
BreathingInhale before unrack. Hold through descent and up to lockout.

Conventional Deadlift

CueDetail
SetupBar over mid-foot. Feet hip-width. Grip just outside legs.
Starting positionHinge to bar. Shins vertical. Scapulae directly over bar. Lats engaged (bend the bar).
BracingBig breath. Brace hard. Squeeze glutes. Bar stays in contact with shins throughout.
DrivePush floor away (leg drive) whilst simultaneously hinging hips forward. Not a straight pull-up.
LockoutStand tall. Hips through. Glutes squeezed. No hyperextension of the lower back.

Nutrition Guidelines

This programme demands fuel. You will not add meaningful size or strength in a caloric deficit. The goal is a modest, controlled surplus — enough to drive muscle growth without excessive fat gain.

GoalTarget
CaloriesMaintenance + 200–400 kcal/day
Protein1.8–2.2g per kg bodyweight (minimum 1.8g)
CarbohydratesFill remaining calories after protein and fat targets
Fat0.8–1g per kg bodyweight
Bodyweight trendAim for 0.25–0.5 kg gain per week

Meal Timing

  • Pre-training: carbohydrate-rich meal 1–2 hours before session.
  • Post-training: protein + carbs within 2 hours. 40–50g protein, 60–80g carbs.
  • Before bed: slow-digesting protein (cottage cheese, casein shake) if total protein is low.
  • Overall daily total matters more than precise timing. Hit your numbers.

Supplement Basics

  • Creatine monohydrate: 5g daily. No loading needed. Take it every day.
  • Protein powder: only if you cannot hit protein targets from food.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2: especially if limited sun exposure.
  • Everything else: unnecessary until the basics are locked in.

What to Do With This Data

  • Bodyweight not moving up after 2 weeks: add 100–200 kcal.
  • Gaining more than 0.6 kg/week: reduce by 100–150 kcal.
  • Lifts stalling for 2+ weeks despite eating: check sleep and stress first, then consider adding a rest day.
  • RPE creeping up on weights that felt easy before: you’re accumulating fatigue. Trust the deload.
