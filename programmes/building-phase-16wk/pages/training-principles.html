Volume Periodisation: The Wave Model

This programme uses an ascending wave model — volume starts moderate and climbs over 3 weeks, then drops on deload week. Each new block starts slightly higher than the previous block's starting point.

RPE & RIR Scale

All working sets use RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) to autoregulate intensity. RIR = Reps In Reserve.

RPERIRDescription
100Maximum effort. Could not do another rep with good form.
9.50–1Might have had one more, but not confident.
91Definitely one more rep left. Hard set.
82Two reps left. Challenging but controlled.
73Three reps left. Working weight, good form throughout.
64+Warm-up territory. Speed work only.
Common mistake: Most lifters train at RPE 9–10 every session and wonder why they plateau. This programme keeps most work at RPE 7–8, with RPE 9+ reserved for peak weeks and the final block. Trust the process.

Progressive Overload in Practice

Overload in this programme comes from three levers — not just adding weight:

  1. Volume: More sets per week as the block progresses.
  2. Load: Small weight increases week to week (1–2.5kg on machines/cables, 2.5–5kg on free weights).
  3. Effort: RPE creeps up from 7 to 9 across a block, even at the same weight.

Do not chase load at the expense of form. A slow, controlled cable fly at RPE 8 builds more muscle than a swinging 30kg dumbbell at RPE 10.

Deload Philosophy: Heavy Compounds

Deload weeks in this programme are not "do nothing" weeks. Instead of dropping volume on isolation work, you swap the entire week to compound-focused, low-rep, heavy work:

  • Strip back to 4–5 exercises per session (all compounds)
  • Work up to heavy singles, doubles, and triples (RPE 8–9)
  • Total volume drops by ~50%, but intensity per set is high
  • This maintains neural drive and strength while letting connective tissue recover from high-rep isolation work
  • You'll often come back to the next block feeling stronger and more responsive to volume
Think of it this way: 3 weeks of bodybuilding → 1 week of powerlifting → repeat. The contrast is the recovery.
