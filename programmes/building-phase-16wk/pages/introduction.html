Section 1: Introduction

Who This Programme Is For

This programme is built for intermediate to advanced lifters (1+ years consistent training) who want to maximise muscle growth over a structured 16-week block. You should already be comfortable in the gym and familiar with the core movement patterns.

It is not a beginner programme. If you've been training less than 12 months, start with the free Foundation Plan at sebastian-fisher.com/free.

Prerequisites: You should be able to perform a full range-of-motion squat, hip hinge, overhead press, and pull-up (or lat pulldown at bodyweight equivalent) before starting this programme.

How to Use This Guide

  1. Read Sections 1–2 first. Understanding the periodisation model and RPE scale is essential.
  2. Follow the blocks in order. Each block builds on the previous. Do not skip ahead.
  3. Use the training logs. Print them or copy to a notebook. Track every session.
  4. Take deload weeks seriously. They are programmed — not optional.
  5. Check the exercise guide for form cues and substitutions before starting.
  6. Adjust nutrition to match the phase. Surplus should increase with volume.

The Arnold Split

This programme uses a 6-day Arnold split — the classic bodybuilding frequency that lets you train each muscle group twice per week with sufficient volume and recovery:

DaySplitPrimary Muscles
MondayChest & BackPecs, lats, rhomboids, traps, rear delts
TuesdayShoulders & ArmsAll 3 delt heads, biceps, triceps, forearms
WednesdayLegsQuads, hamstrings, glutes, calves, adductors
ThursdayChest & BackPecs, lats, rhomboids, traps, rear delts
FridayShoulders & ArmsAll 3 delt heads, biceps, triceps, forearms
SaturdayLegsQuads, hamstrings, glutes, calves, adductors
SundayRestFull recovery day
Why Arnold Split? Each muscle gets hit twice per week — the sweet spot for hypertrophy. Unlike PPL, chest and back are paired together, creating a natural push-pull superset dynamic and keeping sessions balanced. Shoulders & arms get a dedicated day, meaning no fatigue carryover from heavy pressing.
