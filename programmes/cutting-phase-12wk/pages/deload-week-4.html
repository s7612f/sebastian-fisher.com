Deload — Week 4

Reduce total volume by 50%. Focus on compound lifts only at heavy weights and low reps. No accessories. The goal is to maintain neuromuscular activation and let connective tissue and CNS recover before Block 2. Keep calories at deficit — no extra cardio this week.

Deload Protocol

  • Compounds only — drop all isolation and accessory work.
  • 3–5 reps per set at 85–90% of your heaviest working weight.
  • 3 sets per exercise maximum.
  • Rest 2–3 minutes between sets.
  • Sessions should last no more than 35–40 minutes.
  • No HIIT this week. Light walking (20–30 min) is fine.
Day 1 — Chest & Back (Monday)
ExerciseSets × RepsRestNotes
Incline Barbell Press 3 × 5, 4, 3 2–3 min 85–90% of working max. Controlled.
Weighted Pull-Up 3 × 5, 4, 3 2–3 min Heavy. Full dead hang between reps.
Flat Barbell Bench Press 3 × 5, 4, 3 2–3 min Keep technique sharp. No grinding reps.
Day 2 — Shoulders & Arms (Tuesday)
ExerciseSets × RepsRestNotes
Overhead Press (Barbell) 3 × 5, 4, 3 2–3 min Standing. Brace hard.
Close-Grip Bench Press 3 × 5, 4, 3 2–3 min Heavy tricep work. Elbows tucked.
Barbell Curl 3 × 5, 4, 3 2 min Heavy and strict. No momentum.
Day 3 — Legs (Wednesday)
ExerciseSets × RepsRestNotes
Back Squat 3 × 5, 4, 3 3 min Heavy singles optional on final set if feeling strong.
Romanian Deadlift 3 × 5, 4, 3 2–3 min Controlled eccentric. Heavy.
Leg Press 3 × 5, 5, 3 2 min Load it up. Full ROM.

Days 4–6: Repeat Day 1–3 pattern or take additional rest if fatigue is high. Listen to your body. Day 7: Full rest.
