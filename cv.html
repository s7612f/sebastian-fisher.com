01 Statement

From around ten years old I was watching YouTube, drawn to cameras and photography. There was a creator called Kai W who reviewed cameras and explained what each one could do, and I remember watching him and thinking: I want to understand all of this. I was also the kind of kid who took computers apart not to fix them but to understand them. When I got to university I found myself studying philosophy, and it felt like a continuation of school, the expected next step rather than something I had actually chosen. So a year ago I left. I came home and kept editing, because it was the one thing I had never stopped doing, and that instinct has never been just about the creative side: I am as excited about the archive and the pipeline as I am about the edit itself.

Robin Phillips was my piano teacher. I played jazz trumpet. He introduced me to audio gear, and somewhere in those lessons the interest in making things sound right became an interest in making things look right too. Over eight years I cut 30+ productions for his channel, and when his archive kept failing I built him a NAS from scratch. I am now in pre-production on Chasing Chet, a documentary about Chet Baker.

I follow comedy the way some people follow sport. I have watched It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia more than twenty times; there are sixteen seasons. Part of what I am watching for is the craft underneath: the aspect ratios are chosen deliberately, each one controlling exactly what the viewer is given. I am also drawn to the writing, not to write it myself but to break plots apart, study structure, work out why a scene lands. Some of the people on Avalon's roster are ones I already follow. I have built everything I know by myself, and that is both the thing I am most proud of and the reason I want to work inside a proper team. I want to see how a professional operation does this at scale.
