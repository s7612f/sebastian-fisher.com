Section 5: Nutrition

Caloric Surplus Guidelines

Building muscle requires a caloric surplus. Too much and you gain unnecessary fat. Too little and you leave gains on the table.

Training ExperienceSurplusExpected Gain
Beginner (< 1 year)300–500 kcal/day1–1.5 kg/month
Intermediate (1–3 years)200–350 kcal/day0.5–1 kg/month
Advanced (3+ years)150–250 kcal/day0.25–0.5 kg/month
Weigh daily, average weekly. If your weekly average isn't moving up at the expected rate, add 100–150 kcal. If it's climbing too fast, remove 100–150 kcal. Adjust every 2 weeks.

Meal Timing & Training Nutrition

  • Pre-training (1–2 hrs before): Meal with protein + carbs. 30–40g protein, 50–80g carbs.
  • Intra-workout: Optional — 20–30g fast carbs (juice, gummy bears, cyclic dextrin) if sessions exceed 75 minutes.
  • Post-training (within 2 hrs): Protein + carbs. 30–50g protein, 50–100g carbs.
  • Before bed: Slow-digesting protein (casein, cottage cheese, Greek yoghurt).
  • Total meals: 3–5 per day. Spread protein evenly (30–50g per meal).
