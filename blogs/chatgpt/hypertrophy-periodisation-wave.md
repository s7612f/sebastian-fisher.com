Most lifters plateau because they try to add weight every session forever. That works for beginners. It doesn’t work for intermediates and beyond.

Principle: increase training stress progressively across a block, then reduce it (deload), then build again from a higher baseline. Do it with volume, intensity, and frequency — not just more weight.

Practical:
- Pick a 4–6 week mesocycle. Start slightly above your Minimum Effective Volume (MEV). Finish near your Maximum Recoverable Volume (MRV).
- Progress one variable at a time. Week to week, either add 1–2 sets per muscle group or add load while holding sets steady.
- Track per-muscle volume. 10–20 working sets per muscle per week is the productive range for many intermediates when sleep, protein, and execution are in place.
- Deload 5–7 days with 50–60% of normal volume and reduced reps in reserve.

Programme skeleton (upper/lower, 4 days, hypertrophy bias):
- Weeks 1–3: 12 → 16 sets per large muscle per week, 1–3 RIR, moderate load (65–75% 1RM).
- Week 4: Deload.
- Repeat with slightly higher starting volume or load.

Test, record, adjust. The wave is the mechanism.
