Design

References, archives, and resources for studying the design canon. Graphic standards manuals, type specimens, things to print, books to order, and vocabulary for writing about visual culture.

Graphic Standards Manuals

The most important identity systems ever made — fully documented.

  • NASA Graphics Standards Manual (1975) — the "worm" era identity system; covers logo, typography, signage, vehicles, uniforms
  • NASA Graphics Manual (1976) — companion volume with extended applications
  • NYC Transit Authority Graphics Standards Manual — Vignelli / Unimark International, the most studied wayfinding system in design history
  • NJ Transit Graphics Standards Manual — same era, lesser known
  • Bell System Graphic Standards Manual — Saul Bass's 1969 Bell logo system fully documented, one of the most influential identity programmes ever made
  • IBM Logo Manual — Paul Rand's identity system fully documented
  • IBM Graphics 90s–2000 — later IBM visual language
  • Apple Logo Standards (1987) — rainbow era Apple, interesting period document
  • Design Standards Manuals — Federal Designers Guide — meta document on how US government design standards are made and used
  • Corporate Identity Standards (Letterform Archive) — scanned identity manual from the Letterform Archive collection
  • Visual Corporate Identity Manual (Letterform Archive) — another Letterform Archive scanned manual
  • Standards Manual — independent publisher reprinting classic identity systems (NASA, NYC Subway, etc.) as physical books
  • LogoArchive Shop — largest collection of original vintage brand guidelines for sale, mid-century corporate identity programmes

Things to Print

Dense, monochrome, A4-friendly. Technical beauty.

  • Google Patents — search spacecraft, cameras, watches, submarines — vintage patent diagrams print beautifully in black & white
  • NASA Image Library — search "schematic" or "diagram" — Apollo-era technical drawings and mission schematics
  • SkyVector — aviation charts, almost entirely monochrome linework
  • USGS Maps — geological and topographic survey maps, excellent monochrome prints
  • CIA Reading Room — declassified Cold War documents, genuinely good institutional aesthetic
  • Biodiversity Heritage Library — detailed black & white scientific botanical illustrations
  • Bitsavers — IBM, DEC, HP technical manuals; pure institutional typography
  • Archive.org Computer Magazines — vintage computer press, early cyberpunk aesthetic
  • TUHS — Unix Heritage Society — original UNIX documentation, dense monochrome pages
  • Letterform Archive Online — 3,500+ digitised design artefacts, type specimens, posters, ephemera

Type Specimens — Archive.org

The definitive catalogues of letterpress and metal type.

  • 1923 ATF Specimen Book & Catalogue (Hi-Res) — the definitive American Type Founders catalog, dense letterpress specimen pages; best one to start with
  • 1912 American Specimen Book — 1,096 pages, full ATF catalog including machinery and supplies
  • 1898 ATF Desk Book — borders, ornaments, brass rule; oldest of the ATF books
  • 1934 Book of American Types — ATF standard faces, later catalog
  • ATF Typographic Accessories Catalog — ornaments, special characters, initials, monograms
  • Specimen Book of Linotype Faces — Mergenthaler Linotype Company, the Linotype equivalent of the ATF books
  • Mergenthaler 1916 Announcement Specimen — earlier Linotype specimen announcement booklet

Design Inspiration

  • Are.na — search "brutalist typography", "xerox", "institutional design", "Swiss grid" — where serious design people collect references
  • Emigre Magazine Archive — experimental typography journal; important for understanding the intentional-ugly tradition
  • Monoskop — Swiss Graphic Design — archive of Swiss poster and print design
  • Monoskop — Typography — scanned PDFs of Swiss typography books and design theory
  • Design Is History — clean design history reference
  • TM Research Archive — Typographische Monatsblätter journal covers; one of the best things you can browse for tasteful typography

Design Books to Order

Physical books worth owning. The Swiss grid canon.

  • Type: A Visual History Vol. 1 & 2 — Taschen — reprinted specimen sheets 1628–1938; not commentary about them
  • Grid Systems in Graphic Design — Müller-Brockmann — the Swiss grid bible; every page is a reference
  • Typographie — Emil Ruder — Swiss modernism; reads like a technical manual
  • The New Typography — Jan Tschichold — rational layout principles from the 1920s
  • Asymmetric Typography — Jan Tschichold — practical companion to The New Typography
  • An Atlas of Typeforms — Sutton & Bartram — dense catalog-style type reference
  • Pioneers of Modern Typography — Herbert Spencer — documents the key figures; feels like a proper archive document

Reading / Writing Vocabulary

Short books that give you language for writing about design, media, and aesthetics.

  • Capitalist Realism — Mark Fisher — short, punchy; gives you vocabulary around media and aesthetics
  • Understanding Media — Marshall McLuhan — foundational for thinking and writing about media
  • Mythologies — Roland Barthes — short essays; very usable vocabulary for describing culture and design
  • The Society of the Spectacle — Guy Debord — dense; good for building a language around image and representation
