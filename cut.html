THE WRITEUP

PRE-RECOMP — WHAT IT LOOKED LIKE BEFORE

The numbers before Feb 3 are not good. 2.8 sessions per week on average. 22 instances of gaps of 3+ days between sessions. A 63-day complete training break at one point.

Volume was there on the good days — 11.2 sets per session average — but consistency wasn’t. Training happened when motivated. Not when not. That’s a pattern that won’t build anything.

1,451 exercise records across 320 days pre-recomp. Looks like a lot. Spread over nearly a year, it’s not.

WEEKS 1–2 — THE LOCK-IN

From Feb 3: 5.25 sessions per week. 19.7 sets per session. That’s a +88% jump in frequency and +75% more volume per session compared to the pre-recomp baseline. It happened on day one and it did not drop.

Only one gap over 2 days in the entire 36-day block. One. The mechanism was structure — Chest & Back / Shoulders & Arms / Legs / dedicated Arms days — cycling and repeating without gaps.

Day 1 result: incline DB 40kg×20×3. That was the immediate signal the approach was working. Week 2: 330kg leg press mid-travel. Not an excuse week. Not a maintenance week. A training week.

540 exercise records in 36 days. Against 1,451 in 320 days prior. Same person. Different approach.

WEEKS 3–6 — ARM SPECIALISATION

Dominant split became Shoulders & Arms — 174 exercises logged in that category across the recomp. Dedicated Arms/Weak Point sessions added from week 3 onward. Targeted isolation on biceps, triceps, shoulders every 4–5 days.

Volume compounded. Concentration curls added. Cable bicep curls. Triceps extensions with progressive load. Overhead Press went from 40kg to 60kg — that’s a 50% increase on a compound pressing movement in 6 weeks while staying at maintenance calories.

Hammer Curl DB: 32.5kg (pre-recomp) → 35kg (week 6 PR). That’s what progressive overload looks like when the sessions actually happen.

THE RESULT

91.0kg → 89.0kg. Two kilos of fat dropped while staying at maintenance calories.

Half an inch added to the arms in 6 weeks. Pressing strength up 50%. No size lost — size gained.

This is what reacting well to volume actually looks like in the data. The body was under-stimulated before. It responded to the increase immediately and kept responding for the entire block.
