Carbohydrates aren’t magic. They’re useful. Most strength work happens below intensities where glycogen is the limiting factor.

What matters more than headline carb grams:
- Sufficient total calories to recover.
- Adequate protein (2.0–2.4 g/kg) to protect lean mass in a cut.
- Sodium, potassium, magnesium, and hydration.
- Tight execution and honest reps in reserve.

When to raise carbs:
- Very high weekly volume (15–25 hard sets per muscle) with short rest periods.
- Repeated high-output sessions in the same 24h window.

Practical: maintain 40–80 g/day baseline carbs on rest days, push peri‑workout carbs (fruit, honey, rice) on big days. Track performance, not dogma.
