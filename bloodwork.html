BLOODWORK ANALYSIS

Your doctor will tell you whether your numbers are in range. That’s not the same as telling you whether your numbers are optimal for someone who trains seriously. There’s a significant gap between those two things — and most GPs don’t close it.

Send me your panel. I’ll tell you what’s worth addressing, what to leave alone, and what steps to take — whether that’s specific supplementation, dietary changes, or pointing you to the right clinic with the right questions to ask.

WHAT THIS COVERS

► HORMONAL PANEL

Total and free testosterone, LH, FSH, SHBG, estradiol, prolactin. Most males are told they’re “normal” on 12nmol/L testosterone. Whether that’s acceptable for someone training hard is a different question.

► METABOLIC / THYROID

TSH, T3, T4, fasting glucose, HbA1c, insulin. Metabolic health is upstream of body composition, energy, and recovery. If this is off, nothing else will work the way it should.

► LIPIDS & CARDIOVASCULAR

Total cholesterol, HDL, LDL, triglycerides. Relevant to anyone on specific dietary or supplementation protocols. Context matters — a high LDL reading means different things depending on particle size and HDL ratio.

► LIVER & ORGAN MARKERS

ALT, AST, ALP, bilirubin, creatinine, eGFR. Baseline markers that tell you whether the body is handling its current load — training, diet, supplementation — well.

► HAEMATOLOGY / CBC

Full blood count: red cells, white cells, haemoglobin, haematocrit, platelets. Recovery capacity, oxygenation, immune load. Relevant to anyone training hard or managing nutrition at the edges.

► MICRONUTRIENTS

Vitamin D, B12, ferritin, zinc, magnesium. The most common deficiencies in athletes. Each one has a direct effect on performance, mood, recovery, and body composition.

WHAT YOU GET BACK

Not a list of what’s “technically in range”. A clear breakdown of what your panel shows relative to optimal function for a training athlete, and a set of concrete next steps.

  • ► Marker-by-marker breakdown of what’s notable, what’s fine, what needs attention
  • ► Specific supplementation recommendations where applicable — dose, timing, what to expect
  • ► Dietary adjustments that will move the numbers in the right direction
  • ► Clinic recommendations with specific tests to request, if further investigation is warranted
  • ► Context — what the numbers mean relative to your training, diet, and goals

This is not medical advice. It is an informed analysis from someone with direct experience managing complex health markers alongside serious training. What you do with the information is between you and your GP.

DON’T HAVE BLOODWORK YET?

If you haven’t had bloodwork done, getting a full panel is easier and cheaper than most people think. A few options:

MEDICHECKS / THRIVA

UK-based home blood test kits. Finger-prick or venous draw. Full hormonal + metabolic panels available. Results in 24–48h. No GP referral needed.

PRIVATE GP / CLINIC

More comprehensive. Allows face-to-face discussion. I can advise on what to specifically request when you book — most GPs will only order what you ask for.

NHS

Free if your GP agrees to run it. Often limited to what they consider “necessary.” Worth requesting, but don’t rely on it for a full athlete panel.

WHAT TO REQUEST

Message me before you book. I’ll tell you exactly which markers to request for your specific situation — no point paying for a full panel if you only need to check four things.

SUBMIT YOUR PANEL

Fill in the form below with your results or attach a PDF of your panel. Include any context that’s relevant — current training phase, diet approach, any supplementation, health conditions. The more detail, the better the analysis.

I reply within 48 hours. If your results are complex or incomplete, I’ll let you know what additional markers would be useful before giving a full analysis.
