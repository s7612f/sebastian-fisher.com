NUTRITION

Three diet styles. All built around the same principle: enough protein, controlled calories, real food. Pick your approach and stick to it.

STANDARD DIET

Meat, fish, eggs, dairy. The classic bodybuilding diet. Proven for 70 years.

THE ONE RULE: Hit your protein target every day. Everything else is secondary. Aim for 1.8–2.2g protein per kg bodyweight.

VEGETARIAN DIET

No meat or fish. Eggs and dairy are your best friends. The main challenge: hitting protein without fat going too high. Solvable — but requires more planning than the standard diet.

KEY CHALLENGE: Plant proteins are less bioavailable than animal proteins. Aim for 2.2–2.5g protein per kg bodyweight. Leucine content matters — prioritise eggs, dairy, tofu, and soy protein for muscle synthesis.

SUPPLEMENTS FOR VEGETARIANS

  • Creatine monohydrate — non-negotiable. Found mainly in meat; vegetarians are usually deficient.
  • Vitamin B12 — only found in animal products. Supplement daily.
  • Vitamin D3 — if you're in the UK, you need this regardless of diet.
  • Zinc + Iron — reduced absorption from plant sources. Consider a multivitamin.
  • Whey or casein protein powder — easiest way to hit protein targets.

KETOGENIC DIET

Very low carb (under 30–50g/day), high fat, moderate protein. Works for fat loss and some people function well on it. Not ideal for maximum muscle gain — carbs drive insulin and glycogen replenishment which matter for high-volume hypertrophy training. But if it's what works for you, it works.

KETO FOR BODYBUILDING: Best applied during a cut or body recomp phase. If you're trying to add significant mass, carbs are your friend. Cyclical keto (carb refeeds 1–2x/week around training) is a better approach for active lifters.

KETO ADAPTATION PHASE

Weeks 1–3: you'll feel terrible. This is "keto flu" — lethargy, brain fog, reduced gym performance. This is temporary. Stay the course and replenish electrolytes aggressively.

Electrolytes daily: 2,300mg sodium / 3,500mg potassium / 300–500mg magnesium. Bone broth, salt your food heavily, magnesium glycinate before bed.

KETO PROTEIN SOURCES

  • Beef (all cuts) — fatty cuts preferred: ribeye, brisket, mince
  • Eggs — unlimited, the perfect keto food
  • Chicken thighs (with skin) — fattier than breast
  • Salmon, mackerel, sardines — high fat oily fish
  • Bacon, pork belly — traditional keto staples
  • Cheese, butter, cream — add fats easily
  • Nuts — almonds, macadamia, pecans (not cashews — too many carbs)

HIGH-PROTEIN RECIPES

Simple. Fast. High protein. No chef skills required. Tags: [S] = Standard | [V] = Vegetarian | [K] = Keto

GOLDEN ERA BEEF & RICE [S]

Macros per serving: ~700 kcal | 55g protein | 60g carbs | 20g fat
Time: 20 min | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£2
Ingredients:
  • 300g lean beef mince (5–10% fat)
  • 200g cooked rice (about 80g dry)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder
  • Optional: any veg you have
Method:

Brown mince in olive oil over high heat — don't overcrowd the pan. Season with salt, pepper, garlic. Cook 8–10 min until browned. Serve over rice. Done. Meal prep 4 portions at once to save time.

6-EGG OMELETTE [S] [V] [K]

Macros: ~600 kcal | 42g protein | 3g carbs | 46g fat
Time: 10 min | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£1.20
Ingredients:
  • 6 whole eggs
  • 50g cheese (cheddar)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Salt, pepper, chilli flakes
  • Optional: spinach, mushrooms, peppers
Method:

Whisk eggs with salt and pepper. Melt butter in pan over medium heat. Pour in eggs — don't stir. Once edges set, add fillings and cheese. Fold in half. Done in 4 minutes once the pan's hot. Old-school golden era staple — cheap, fast, perfect macros.

COTTAGE CHEESE PROTEIN BOWL [V]

Macros: ~400 kcal | 45g protein | 35g carbs | 8g fat
Time: 5 min | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£1.50
Ingredients:
  • 300g low-fat cottage cheese
  • 1 banana
  • 30g oats (raw)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Optional: berries, cinnamon
Method:

Mix everything in a bowl. Eat. No cooking. No excuses. Good pre-workout meal or breakfast. The casein in cottage cheese is slow-digesting — also excellent before bed to support overnight recovery.

SMASH BURGER PROTEIN BOWL [S] [K]

Macros: ~650 kcal | 50g protein | 8g carbs | 45g fat (keto) / add bun for +200 kcal
Time: 15 min | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£2.50
Ingredients:
  • 250g 80/20 beef mince — formed into patties
  • 2 slices cheddar
  • 2 eggs (fried)
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp butter
Method:

Cast iron pan on high heat until smoking. Place patties in — smash flat immediately. Cook 2 min, flip, add cheese. Fry eggs separately. Stack and eat. No bun needed if keto. Add one if bulking hard.

TOFU & RICE BULK BOWL [V]

Macros: ~650 kcal | 40g protein | 65g carbs | 18g fat
Time: 20 min | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£1.80
Ingredients:
  • 280g firm tofu — pressed and cubed
  • 200g cooked rice
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • Any veg (broccoli, courgette, edamame)
Method:

Press tofu to remove moisture (30 min under a weight, or just squeeze it hard). Pan-fry cubes in sesame oil on high until golden and crisp — don't move them too early. Add garlic, soy, veg. Serve over rice. Key: get the tofu actually crispy — it makes all the difference.

SALMON & EGGS — POST-WORKOUT [S] [K]

Macros: ~550 kcal | 58g protein | 0g carbs | 35g fat
Time: 15 min | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£3
Ingredients:
  • 200g salmon fillet
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Salt, pepper, lemon
  • Handful spinach
Method:

Season salmon, pan fry skin-down in butter 4 min, flip 3 min. Scramble eggs separately. Wilted spinach takes 1 min. Classic post-training meal. Omega-3s from salmon aid recovery — this is the kind of meal golden era athletes actually ate.

OVERNIGHT OATS FOR BULKING [V]

Macros: ~750 kcal | 50g protein | 95g carbs | 15g fat
Time: 5 min prep (night before) | Servings: 1 | Cost: ~£1
Ingredients:
  • 100g rolled oats
  • 300ml whole milk
  • 30g protein powder (unflavoured or vanilla)
  • 1 banana
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter
Method:

Mix oats, milk, protein powder in a jar. Refrigerate overnight. In the morning: top with banana and peanut butter. No cooking, high calories, actually tastes good. Prep 5 at a time on Sunday and you're done for the week.
