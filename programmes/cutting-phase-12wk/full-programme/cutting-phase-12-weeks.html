Cutting Phase

12-Week Fat Loss Programme
Compound-First · Pyramid Loading · No Fluff

Programme Overview

This is a 12-week structured fat-loss programme built for intermediate-to-advanced lifters who want to strip body fat whilst preserving as much muscle and strength as possible. Six training days per week using an Arnold split, with strategic deload weeks and a final taper to transition into a reverse diet.

Training split: Arnold Split — Chest & Back / Shoulders & Arms / Legs / Chest & Back / Shoulders & Arms / Legs (6 days/week)

Session length: 50–65 minutes

Equipment needed: Barbell, dumbbells, cables, pull-up bar, leg press

Rest days: Sunday (mandatory)

Rep scheme: Pyramid — 5 sets of 15, 12, 8, 8, 6 (ascending load, descending reps)

How to Use This Programme

  • Track every session in a logbook — write sets, reps, weight used, and RPE.
  • Pyramid loading: increase weight each set as reps decrease. The set of 15 is a warm-up/feeder set; the sets of 6–8 are your working weight.
  • RPE targets are given per block. RPE 8 = 2 reps left in the tank. RPE 9 = 1 rep left.
  • Deload weeks (4 & 8): compounds only, heavy low-rep (3–5 reps), reduce volume by 50%.
  • Nutrition: aim for a 400–600 kcal deficit. Minimum 2.2g protein per kg bodyweight.
  • Cardio: 2–3 HIIT sessions per week, 15–20 minutes, on non-leg days or post-session.
  • If strength drops more than 10% on any compound, add a refeed day or reduce deficit.

Signs You Need to Pull Back

  • Strength declining week-on-week on compounds (not just a bad day).
  • Persistent joint pain that worsens during sessions.
  • Sleep quality tanking — waking unrested, insomnia.
  • Mood and motivation consistently low for 5+ consecutive days.
  • Resting heart rate elevated 8–10 bpm above baseline.

Action: Take an extra rest day, add a refeed, or reduce deficit by 200 kcal. Do not push through — you will lose muscle.

Target RPE: 7–8 on working sets (sets of 8 and 6).
Deficit: 400–500 kcal below maintenance.
Cardio: 2 × HIIT sessions, 15 minutes.

Deload — Week 4

Reduce total volume by 50%. Focus on compound lifts only at heavy weights and low reps. No accessories. The goal is to maintain neuromuscular activation and let connective tissue and CNS recover before Block 2. Keep calories at deficit — no extra cardio this week.

Deload Protocol

  • Compounds only — drop all isolation and accessory work.
  • 3–5 reps per set at 85–90% of your heaviest working weight.
  • 3 sets per exercise maximum.
  • Rest 2–3 minutes between sets.
  • Sessions should last no more than 35–40 minutes.
  • No HIIT this week. Light walking (20–30 min) is fine.

Block 2: Weeks 5–7 — Volume Maintenance

Maintain training volume to preserve muscle mass whilst the deficit deepens. Slightly higher intensity on compounds (RPE 8–9). Same exercises, but we begin tracking weekly set changes to auto-regulate based on recovery. If you cannot hit the prescribed sets at the given RPE, drop one set.

Target RPE: 8–9 on compounds, 8 on accessories.
Deficit: 500–600 kcal below maintenance.
Cardio: 2–3 × HIIT sessions, 15–20 minutes.
Refeed: 1 day per week — bump carbs to maintenance (see Refeed section).

Volume Auto-Regulation

  • The tables below show target sets for Weeks 5, 6, and 7.
  • If RPE consistently exceeds target by 0.5+ across a session, drop 1 set from each isolation exercise the following week.
  • If you complete all prescribed sets at or below target RPE, maintain volume.
  • Compound sets are fixed — do not drop compound volume. Drop isolation first.

Deload — Week 8

Same protocol as Week 4. Compounds only, heavy and low-rep, minimal volume. You are now 8 weeks into a deficit — fatigue is accumulated and hormonal adaptations are real. This deload is critical. Do not skip it.

Deficit Fatigue Check

  • If you have lost more than 1% of bodyweight per week on average, your deficit is too aggressive. Reduce by 200 kcal.
  • If compound strength has dropped more than 10% from Block 1, add a second refeed day this week.
  • If libido, mood, and energy are all significantly impaired, consider a full diet break (1 week at maintenance) instead of a standard deload.

Block 3: Weeks 9–11 — Intensity

Final training block before the taper. Volume drops further but intensity is at its highest. Introduce intensity techniques (drop sets, rest-pause, slow eccentrics) on the final set of isolation exercises to squeeze every last bit of stimulus from fewer total sets. Compound strength maintenance is THE priority — if your squat, bench, and deadlift hold steady, you are keeping muscle.

Target RPE: 9 on compounds, 8–9 on accessories.
Deficit: 500–600 kcal below maintenance.
Cardio: 3 × HIIT sessions, 15–20 minutes. Can add 1–2 × 30-min LISS if fat loss has stalled.
Refeed: 1–2 days per week depending on fatigue.

Overtraining in a Deficit — Red Flags

  • Compound lifts down 15%+ from Block 1 — take a diet break.
  • Persistent DOMS lasting 4+ days in the same muscle group.
  • Elevated resting heart rate, disrupted sleep, frequent illness.
  • Grip strength noticeably weaker — often an early CNS fatigue sign.
  • Action: Drop to 4 training days, add a refeed, or take 3–5 days at maintenance calories.

Week 12 — Taper & Reverse Diet Prep

The final week. Training volume drops to minimum effective dose. The goal is not to push hard — it is to maintain neural patterns, let your body recover, and prepare for the transition out of the deficit. You will begin reversing calories this week.

Week 12 Protocol

  • Train 4 days this week (Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu), then rest Fri–Sun.
  • Compounds only, 3 sets of 6–8 reps at RPE 7–8.
  • No intensity techniques. No drop sets. No rest-pause.
  • No HIIT this week. Light walking only.
  • Begin reverse diet: add 150–200 kcal (primarily carbohydrates) to daily intake.
  • Take final progress photos and measurements on Sunday morning (fasted).

Reverse Diet Protocol (Post-Cut)

  • Add 150–200 kcal per week, primarily from carbohydrates.
  • Maintain protein at 2.0–2.2g/kg throughout the reverse.
  • Continue training at Block 1 volume and RPE 7–8 for 2–3 weeks.
  • Monitor weight: expect 1–2kg of water/glycogen regain in the first week. This is normal.
  • Once at maintenance calories for 2+ weeks, you can begin a building phase.

Cardio & Conditioning Protocol

Cardio during a cut serves one purpose: to increase energy expenditure without adding excessive fatigue. It is a tool, not the driver. The deficit comes from nutrition. Cardio widens the gap.

HIIT Protocol (2–3 × per week)

  • Duration: 15–20 minutes total (including warm-up and cool-down).
  • Format: 20–30 seconds all-out effort, 60–90 seconds recovery. Repeat 8–12 rounds.
  • Modality: Assault bike, rowing machine, hill sprints, or battle ropes. Avoid running if joint issues exist.
  • Timing: Post-session (after weights) or on separate occasions. Never before legs.
  • Schedule: Tues/Thurs/Sat or Mon/Wed/Fri — avoid doing HIIT on leg days.

LISS Protocol (Optional, Block 3 Only)

  • Duration: 25–35 minutes.
  • Intensity: Heart rate 120–140 bpm. You should be able to hold a conversation.
  • Modality: Incline treadmill walk, light cycling, or swimming.
  • Frequency: 1–2 × per week, only if fat loss has stalled and you have already maximised deficit from nutrition.
  • Do not add LISS unless fat loss has genuinely plateaued for 10+ days at the prescribed deficit.

Cardio Mistakes to Avoid

  • Do not do more than 3 HIIT sessions per week. Recovery is already compromised in a deficit.
  • Do not do HIIT before a leg session. Ever.
  • Do not use cardio to "make up" for a bad eating day. Fix the nutrition instead.
  • If adding cardio causes strength to drop, remove it and increase the dietary deficit instead.

Nutrition Guidelines

Your diet is the engine of this programme. The training preserves muscle; the nutrition drives fat loss. Get this wrong and you will either lose muscle (deficit too aggressive, protein too low) or not lose fat at all (deficit too small, adherence too poor).

Step 3: Meal Timing

Recommended Meal Structure

  • Meal 1 (Morning): Protein + fat focused. Eggs, Greek yoghurt, or protein shake with nut butter.
  • Meal 2 (Pre-Training, 2hrs before): Protein + carbs. Chicken/fish with rice or oats.
  • Meal 3 (Post-Training, within 1hr): Protein + carbs. Highest carb meal of the day. Whey shake + banana, then whole food within 90 min.
  • Meal 4 (Evening): Protein + fat + vegetables. Steak/salmon with greens and olive oil.
  • Meal 5 (Optional, before bed): Casein protein or cottage cheese. Slow-digesting protein to minimise overnight catabolism.

Nutrition Red Lines

  • Never drop protein below 2.0g/kg. Muscle loss accelerates rapidly below this threshold.
  • Never drop fat below 0.7g/kg for more than 2 weeks. Hormonal disruption is real.
  • Never drop total calories below BMR (basal metabolic rate). Approximately bodyweight (kg) × 20–22 kcal.
  • If you are consistently hungry, irritable, and cold, your deficit is too aggressive. Add calories.

Refeed Day Strategy

A refeed day is a planned, controlled increase in carbohydrate intake to temporarily bring calories up to (or near) maintenance. The purpose is to replenish glycogen, support leptin levels, improve training performance, and provide a psychological break from the deficit.

Refeed Protocol

  • Frequency: 1 day per week in Blocks 1–2. Up to 2 days per week in Block 3 if needed.
  • Timing: Best placed the day before your hardest training day (e.g., before leg day).
  • Calories: Bring total intake up to maintenance (or within 100 kcal of it).
  • Macros: Keep protein the same. Keep fat the same or slightly lower. Add all extra calories as carbohydrates.
  • Carb sources: Rice, pasta, potatoes, oats, bread, fruit, cereal. Low fibre, high glycaemic is fine on refeed days.
  • This is not a cheat day. It is structured, tracked, and intentional.

Signs You Need a Refeed

  • Training performance dropping (flat, weak, no pump).
  • Feeling cold, especially hands and feet.
  • Sleep quality declining.
  • Constantly thinking about food (mental preoccupation).
  • Weight loss stalled for 7+ days despite adherence.

Substitution Rules

  • Never substitute a compound movement with an isolation movement (e.g., do not replace Bench Press with Cable Fly).

  • If you cannot perform any squat or deadlift variant pain-free, consult a physiotherapist before continuing.

How to Read Your Check-In

  • Weight dropping, strength stable: Perfect. Keep going.
  • Weight dropping, strength dropping: Deficit may be too aggressive. Add a refeed or reduce deficit by 200 kcal.
  • Weight stable, strength stable: Deficit is too small. Remove 200 kcal or add 1 HIIT session.
  • Weight stable, strength dropping: Recovery issue. Check sleep, stress, and protein intake first.
  • Waist shrinking but scale is flat: Likely recomposition. Do not panic. Trust the waist measurement.

Progress Photo Protocol

  • Front relaxed, front double bicep, side relaxed, back lat spread.
  • Same lighting, same location, same distance from camera.
  • Compare photos side-by-side at the end of the 12 weeks — not week to week.
